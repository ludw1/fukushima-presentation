1. Nuclear Fission Basics

hub

Chain Reaction

Neutrons split fissile nuclei, releasing energy and more neutrons, propagating a self-sustaining reaction.

local_fire_department

Decay Heat

Even after fission stops (reactor shutdown), radioactive byproducts continue to generate intense heat that requires active cooling.

electric_bolt

Power Generation

The heat boils water into steam, which spins a turbine connected to a generator to produce electricity.

3. Chernobyl Design

RBMK-1000 [4]

Graphite Moderated

Uses solid graphite blocks to moderate neutrons. This allowed using cheaper, un-enriched uranium.

Coolant

Light water (H₂O). Steam from the heated water is directly used to power the turbines.

Lack of Containment

Housed in a standard industrial building rather than a massive reinforced concrete containment dome.

warning

gpp_bad Fatal Flaw

Positive Void Coefficient

If the cooling water boils into steam (a 'void'), it absorbs fewer neutrons but the graphite keeps moderating them. Less water = More neutrons = Power spikes = More boiling.

[3]

4. Chernobyl Accident

26.04.1986 [5]
Release: ~5,300 PBq [7]

Pre-Test Setup

To maintain power after an unintended drop, operators removed almost all control rods, leaving the reactor highly unstable.

01:23:04 - Reduction of Flow

The test begins. Reduction of water flow leads to water boiling off faster, increasing reactivity due to the positive void coefficient.

01:23:40 - SCRAM & Power Surge

Emergency shutdown (AZ-5) initiated, but control rods jam. The rods' graphite tips displace water, causing a massive power surge instead of stopping it.

~01:24:00 - Explosions

Uncontrolled reaction causes a steam and hydrogen explosion inside the core, blowing the 1000-ton lid off.

Aftermath

The exposed graphite catches fire, burning for 10 days and continuously spreading radioactive material over a huge area.

[6]

5. Fukushima Design

BWR

2 Fuel Core Element

Light water acts as BOTH coolant and moderator. If water boils away, moderation stops, naturally halting the chain reaction.

3 Control Rod Element

Inserted from the bottom to absorb neutrons and control the reaction rate.

1 & 17 Containment

The core is inside a thick steel reactor vessel (1), sitting within a massive concrete chamber (17).

waves

warning Fatal Flaw

Infrastructure Placement

Emergency diesel generators required to pump cooling water were placed in low-lying basements vulnerable to flooding.

[8]

6. Fukushima Accident

11.03.2011
Release: ~520 PBq [7]

14:46 - The Earthquake

A 9.0 magnitude earthquake strikes. Reactors shut down automatically (SCRAM). Fission stops, but decay heat remains high.

15:35 - The Tsunami

A massive tsunami floods the backup diesel generators. Complete Station Blackout occurs; cooling water stops flowing.

Next 3 Days - Meltdown

Water boils off. Exposed fuel rods melt. The extreme heat causes the zirconium cladding to react with steam, creating Hydrogen gas.

Building Explosions

Hydrogen gas is vented into the upper buildings, where it explodes, destroying the roofs. Primary containments remain mostly intact.

[9]
